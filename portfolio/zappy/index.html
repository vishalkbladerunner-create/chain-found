Marketplace

Zappy

A high-velocity P2P card marketplace built on proven collector demand — moving fast where legacy platforms move slow.

Zappy is a peer-to-peer marketplace purpose-built for card traders. On-chain ownership turns every card into a verifiable, tradeable asset — adding provenance, fractional ownership, and cross-border liquidity to a market that has always been hampered by geography and trust.

How a trade clears

Five steps. Sub-second settlement.

From listing to settlement, every step lives on the same rails — no escrow agent, no centralised custody, no settlement delay.

01
List

Sellers list cards with photos, condition, and asking price. Each listing mints a soulbound provenance record.

02
Verify

Verified-by-Zappy oracle confirms physical authenticity and condition. Buyers see exactly what they're getting.

03
Match

Order book matches buyers and sellers P2P — no middleman, no escrow service, no platform inventory.

04
Settle

Sub-second on-chain settlement. Card ownership transfers immediately; physical ships next.

05
Track

Every trade strengthens the proof-of-trade graph. Provenance, price history, and ownership accrue to the protocol.

Inside the thesis

Why Zappy.

01

The Market

Trading card culture is one of the fastest-growing collectibles categories globally — billions in annual volume sitting on top of primitive infrastructure. Sellers are throttled by escrow, geography, and platform rake.

02

The Web3 Layer

The 12-week sprint focuses on the on-chain trading layer, custody, and marketplace liquidity mechanics built around verified provenance. Cards become composable assets with on-chain history and instant settlement.

03

The Velocity

P2P matching, sub-second settlement, and a fee structure built for traders — not collectors. Zappy is designed to move at the speed of crypto-native markets, not legacy collectibles platforms.

04

The Network

Every transaction strengthens the proof-of-trade graph: provenance, ownership history, and price discovery accrue to the protocol — not to a custodial intermediary.
